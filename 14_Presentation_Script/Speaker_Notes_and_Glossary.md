# Speaker Notes & Q&A Glossary — Limber Capstone
### Plain-language definitions to keep the room with you and to answer faculty Q&A with confidence · June 2026
> Say the term, then gloss it in a few words. Below: the gloss for the talk + a deeper version for Q&A.

## Reimbursement & the DiGA / FDA point (the densest line in the dossier)
**The one-line version (for the audience):**
> "The best way to make money here isn't charging users — it's getting an insurer to pay for the app as a covered treatment. Germany already does this; the US route gives prestige but not payment — so we treat it as a credibility play, not early revenue."

**Term by term (for Q&A):**
- **Reimbursement** — an insurer or government health system pays for your product on the user's behalf (like a covered prescription), instead of the user paying out of pocket. It's the holy grail because it removes the price barrier that drives churn.
- **Wedge** — a narrow, strategic entry point into a market.
- **DiGA** (*Digitale Gesundheitsanwendungen*) — Germany's program where doctors can **prescribe approved health apps** and **public insurers pay** for them. ~58 apps are listed; a 90-day "course" reimburses at a mean of **~€227**. ("Course" = a defined treatment period.)
- **The "payer problem"** — you can build a clinically-proven app, but with no insurer willing to *pay*, you have no revenue. **Pear Therapeutics** got FDA clearance but couldn't secure reimbursement and **went bankrupt in 2023**. Germany's DiGA "solved" this.
- **FDA De Novo** — a US Food & Drug Administration pathway that authorizes a **brand-new type of medical device** (no existing equivalent). It confers strong credibility ("FDA-authorized").
- **EndeavorRx / Akili** — EndeavorRx is the first **FDA-authorized video-game treatment** (kids' ADHD); its maker **Akili**, once valued near **$1B**, struggled commercially and **sold for ~$34M** — proof that FDA approval ≠ commercial success.
- **Limber's stance:** pursue DiGA/clinical reimbursement in Europe as a **v2 credibility + trust move**, never as v1 revenue.

## Moat
**Gloss (for the audience):** "the one advantage a competitor can't copy."
**Q&A depth:** a *moat* (Buffett's term) is a **durable competitive advantage** — brand/trust, network effects, switching costs, cost advantage, or proprietary tech/data. Limber's moat is **trust through evidence-honesty**: a rival can clone our features in a weekend, but they can't clone our honesty without giving up the overclaims their business depends on. The experts trust no app yet — whoever earns that trust first owns the position.

## FTC
**Gloss:** "the U.S. Federal Trade Commission — the agency that polices false advertising."
**Q&A depth:** in 2016 the FTC charged **Lumosity** with deceptive ads (claims that its games sharpen everyday cognition / prevent dementia) **without adequate scientific evidence**; Lumosity settled for **$2M**. It's the cautionary tale that makes evidence-honesty a defensible strategy.

## Far transfer
**Gloss:** "getting better at the game doesn't make you better at life."
**Q&A depth:** *near transfer* (improvement on the trained task and very similar tasks) is real; *far transfer* (improvement to general intelligence or real-world cognition) is what's essentially **null** across the rigorous meta-analyses (Simons 2016; Sala & Gobet 2019). Limber therefore claims **only near-transfer + lifestyle support**, never IQ gains.

## Kleim & Jones / neuroplasticity
**Gloss:** "the rules for how the brain actually rewires."
**Q&A depth:** Kleim & Jones (2008) codified **10 principles of experience-dependent plasticity** — specificity, intensity, repetition, salience, timing, age, etc. *Neuroplasticity* = the brain's ability to change its structure/function with experience; real, lifelong, but **specific, effortful, and slower with age**. Limber's **Prime → Train → Consolidate** loop is built directly on these principles.

## ~85% "challenge zone"
**Gloss:** "hard enough to grow, not so hard you quit."
**Q&A depth:** consistent with *desirable-difficulty* research (Bjork; Kornell & Bjork 2008) — keeping accuracy around ~85% maximizes learning; too easy = no adaptation, too hard = disengagement. Limber's adaptive engine holds you there.

## TAM / SAM / SOM — market sizing (if the business slide is questioned)
**Gloss (for the audience):** "biggest-possible market → the slice we can actually serve → what we'll realistically capture."

**The three nested circles:**
- **TAM** (Total Addressable Market) = everyone who could ever use it, if all paid — the theoretical ceiling. **~$58B.**
- **SAM** (Serviceable Available Market) = the slice we can actually reach & who can pay (product, language, channel, income). **~$8.8B.**
- **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market) = what we can realistically capture near-term given competition/execution — *the number we plan against.* **~$103M Year-3 ARR** (inside the modelled 0.5–2% of SAM = $44–176M band).

**The inputs behind the numbers (bottom-up = population × price):**
- **Population:** ~**730M** cognitively-concerned, digitally-active adults (TAM) → narrowed to ~**110M** income-qualified, motivation-matched (SAM).
- **Pricing (ARPU = Average Revenue Per User / yr):** ~**$80/yr** blended B2C; **$35–80 PEPM** for employers (*PEPM = Per Employee Per Month*, the standard enterprise-wellbeing price unit); **€200–500** per DTx course; **$500–2,500** per longevity-clinic user.
- **Math:** 730M × ~$80 ≈ **$58B TAM**; 110M × ~$80 ≈ **$8.8B SAM**; 0.5–2% of SAM over yr 3–5 ≈ **$44–176M ARR (SOM)**.

**Key terms / why it matters:**
- **Bottom-up vs top-down:** *bottom-up* builds the number from our own units (population × price) — defensible; *top-down* takes a slice of an analyst's market figure — weaker. We use bottom-up as primary and cross-check against analyst reports. **Never plan off TAM** ("we just need 1%" is a red flag) — plan off **SOM**.
- **ARR** = Annual Recurring Revenue (predictable yearly subscription revenue).
- **[M]** confidence = Medium (reasoned estimates, not audited figures — deliberately humble).

**One-liner if pressed:** "If everyone who cares about their brain and is online used Limber, that's ~$58B (TAM); the reachable, able-to-pay slice is ~$8.8B (SAM); capturing 0.5–2% of that over five years is ~$44–176M ARR (SOM) — and our model lands at ~$103M, right in that band."
