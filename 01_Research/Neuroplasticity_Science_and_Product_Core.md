# Neuroplasticity — The Science Core (Global)
### The scientific spine of Limber · evidence-graded · June 2026
> Produced by a global research team (mechanisms, specialist frameworks, clinical efficacy, product teardown). **Neuroplasticity is the core of the product, so the science must be unimpeachable.** Every claim is graded **Strong / Moderate / Weak** and we deliberately surface null results, retractions, and over-claims — because the category's defining failure (Lumosity's $2M FTC fine) was overclaiming. *Limber's moat is being the honest one.*

---

## 0. The one-paragraph thesis
The brain remains plastic across the lifespan, but plasticity is **gated, specific, effortful, and slow** — not magic. Decades of work (Merzenich, Pascual-Leone, Boyd, Davidson) and the field's translational backbone — **Kleim & Jones' 10 Principles of Experience-Dependent Plasticity (2008)** — converge on a clear, defensible recipe: *attended, progressively-difficult, repeated, spaced, personally-meaningful practice*, sitting on top of the **lifestyle levers that gate plasticity biochemically** (aerobic exercise → BDNF, sleep consolidation, stress regulation, novelty). Limber productizes exactly this recipe — and refuses the claims the evidence won't support (far-transfer to general intelligence, "rewire your brain in 21 days," structural change from a meditation streak).

## 1. Mechanisms (current, 2023–2026)
- **Synaptic (LTP/LTD)** — NMDA-gated Ca²⁺ drives AMPA-receptor trafficking; LTP/LTD interact synergistically; durable change needs protein synthesis (synaptic tag-and-capture). **[Strong]**
- **Metaplasticity & homeostasis** — the BCM "sliding threshold" and synaptic scaling keep plasticity stable yet specific — i.e., the brain resists runaway change, which is *why consistent practice over time beats cramming.* **[Strong]**
- **Structural** — spine remodeling (days), gray-matter MRI change (6–12+ weeks of intensive practice), myelination (months–years). **No basis for "21-day rewiring."** **[Strong]**
- **Molecular gates** — **BDNF** (TrkB) is the permissive signal for lasting LTP; **dopamine** acts as a delayed "eligibility trace" converting candidate changes into durable ones (novelty/reward); **acetylcholine** sharpens encoding under attention; **norepinephrine** sets arousal/gain. *Plasticity requires a permissive neurochemical state — attention and arousal are not optional.* **[Strong]**
- **Perineuronal nets (PNNs)** — molecular "brakes" that densify with age; dynamically regulate adult plasticity. Pharmacological reopening (chondroitinase, valproate) is experimental and **not** consumer-feasible — behavioral levers are the realistic path. **[Strong in animals / Moderate in humans]**
- **Glia** — astrocytes (D-serine for LTP), microglia (synaptic pruning) are now active plasticity partners. **[Strong, advancing]**
- **Adult neurogenesis** — genuinely unresolved in humans (2025 scRNA-seq vs histology debate). **Do NOT build claims on human neurogenesis.** **[Moderate, contested]**

## 2. The levers that reliably drive adaptive plasticity (evidence-graded)
| Lever | Grade | Honest scope for the product |
|---|---|---|
| **Focused attention / deliberate practice** | **Strong** | The gate. Passive exposure ≠ plasticity. Every feature must demand active, effortful engagement. |
| **Aerobic exercise → BDNF** | **Strong (BDNF) / Moderate (structure)** | 30–40 min, 60–70% HRmax, 3–4×/wk; BDNF window ~1–2h post-exercise lowers LTP threshold. Pair movement with training. |
| **Sleep & spaced learning** | **Strong** | Sleep is the consolidation engine (SO–spindle–ripple coupling). Spacing exploits reconsolidation. Treat both as *features*. |
| **Stress regulation** | **Strong** | Chronic cortisol atrophies hippocampus/PFC, grows amygdala — it *closes* the plasticity window. Regulation is a prerequisite, not "wellness." |
| **Novelty** | **Strong (mechanism)** | Novelty drives VTA dopamine → lowers LTP threshold. Vary tasks/contexts; predictable repetition dampens the signal. |
| **CBT cognitive restructuring** | **Strong (functional)** | Documented PFC–limbic functional change. Say "functional," never "structural." |
| **Mindfulness / focused-attention** | **Moderate (function) / Weak (structure)** | Reliable stress/attention benefits; **Kral 2022 (n=218 RCT) found NO structural change; a 2023 meta-analysis was RETRACTED (2025).** Never claim "rewires the brain." |
| **Breathwork / HRV / cold** | **Moderate (acute) / Weak (plasticity)** | Autonomic *primers* that set state before practice — not plasticity drivers in themselves. |
| **Diet/fasting, light/circadian** | **Weak–Moderate** | Enabling context (BDNF, circadian gating); modest; don't headline. |

## 3. The translational backbone — Kleim & Jones (2008) 10 Principles
The most-cited framework for turning plasticity science into design. Limber's product is built on these:
1. **Use it or lose it** · 2. **Use it and improve it** · 3. **Specificity** (train the named circuit; don't promise general gains) · 4. **Repetition matters** · 5. **Intensity matters** (effortful) · 6. **Time matters** (spacing, early) · 7. **Salience matters** (personal meaning is a *biological* lever) · 8. **Age matters** · 9. **Transference** (related circuits; ≠ far transfer) · 10. **Interference** (maladaptive habits compete with new learning).

## 4. Specialist frameworks Limber stands on
- **Merzenich** — adult cortical maps remodel with *attended, rewarded, progressive* practice (the BrainHQ evidence base). **[Strong]**
- **Pascual-Leone** — practice + (optional) stimulation; mental rehearsal produces real map change; "brain reserve" pillars (cognitive/physical/social/emotional). **[Strong]**
- **Lara Boyd** — chemical→structural→functional change; *difficulty drives structural change*; individual variability is the signal, not noise. **[Strong]**
- **Davidson** — well-being is trainable: Awareness, Connection, Insight, Purpose. **[Moderate–Strong]**
- **Suzuki / Diamond / Farb** — exercise→hippocampus→memory; executive function is trainable; interoception underpins self-regulation. **[Strong / Strong / Moderate]**
- **Use with caution** — Doidge (n=1 cases), Huberman (separate peer-reviewed light/breathing/sleep from podcast supplement claims), Swart (applied, borrowed). The *underlying* scientists, not the popularizers, anchor Limber.

## 5. What the evidence forbids (the honesty firewall)
- **Far transfer is null** (Simons 2016 consensus; Sala & Gobet 2019 second-order meta-analysis: far-transfer variance ≈ 0). Near transfer (better at the trained task) is robust; general-intelligence/life-outcome claims are not.
- **Mindfulness structural "rewiring"** — unsupported in RCT (Kral 2022); the supporting meta-analysis was **retracted**.
- **Neurofeedback "treats ADHD"** — fully-blinded assessments show ~null (SMD ≈0.04, 95% CI −0.10–0.18); a small protocol-dependent signal appears with established standard protocols (SMD ≈0.21), but much of the apparent benefit is expectancy/placebo. Say "studied for attention," not "treats."
- **Growth mindset** "improves grades" — d≈0.05–0.14 (Macnamara & Burgoyne 2023, corrected estimate near the low end once publication bias is adjusted); replications weak.
- **Lumosity precedent** — the FTC requires "competent and reliable scientific evidence" (well-controlled human studies) for cognition/disease claims. This is the legal floor Limber designs to.

## 7. The product core — "Prime → Train → Consolidate" (neuroplasticity, operationalized)
Limber's daily loop is the Kleim & Jones recipe made usable:
- **PRIME (open the window).** Set state + attention: a brief regulation step (breath/HRV or movement nudge) + a clear, personally-meaningful goal tether. *Mechanism: parasympathetic regulation restores PFC; attention/ACh gates encoding; salience (Principle 7) is biological.* **[Strong]**
- **TRAIN (drive the change).** One **specific**, **adaptive-difficulty** deliberate-practice session held near **~85% accuracy** (the challenge zone consistent with *desirable-difficulty* research, Bjork; Kornell & Bjork 2008), with genuine **novelty** and effortful engagement. *Mechanism: specificity + intensity + attention + dopamine/novelty.* **[Strong]**
- **CONSOLIDATE (lock it in).** **Spaced** review scheduling + **sleep** support + brief reflection. *Mechanism: reconsolidation + SO–spindle–ripple consolidation.* **[Strong]**
Wrapped around the loop: the **lifestyle levers** (move, sleep, stress, novelty) treated as first-class plasticity inputs, **honest baseline assessments** every 4 weeks (processing speed, working memory, attention, inhibition), **adaptive difficulty** held near **~85% accuracy** (consistent with *desirable-difficulty* research — Bjork 1994; Kornell & Bjork 2008 — not a single landmark RCT), and **personalization** for individual variability (BDNF Val66Met logic: the Met allele is carried by ~20–50% of people depending on ancestry and blunts activity-dependent BDNF — so uniform protocols are scientifically wrong).

## 8. Design guardrails (avoid *maladaptive* plasticity — Principle 10)
No punitive streaks (entrench guilt loops); reflection that closes loops (not rumination); progressive spaced challenge (Yerkes-Dodson); predictable (non-variable-ratio) notifications; mastery/meaning over compulsion. *Self-compassion and autonomy are themselves PFC-supportive.*
