## 0. Executive summary (what matters)
1. **Big, fast, validated demand.** Mental-health apps ~**USD 9.94B (2025) → 22.73B (2030) @ ~18% CAGR** (MarketsandMarkets); neurotechnology ~**USD 15–18B (2025) → ~53B (2034) @ ~13–14%**; brain-fitness software ~**USD 5–7B** growing ~7%+; biohacking/longevity ~**USD 56B (2026) @ ~25%**. Cognitive enhancement is the #1 biohacking use-case (77%). **[M/H]**
2. **The category's defining wound is credibility.** Lumosity's **$2M FTC** settlement and the 70-scientist 2014 consensus poisoned trust; the honest, evidence-graded player wins. **[H]**
3. **A real white space exists:** *clinically-honest, personalized neuroplasticity with feedback at consumer price and no clinician dependency* — BrainHQ has the evidence but no biofeedback; Muse has feedback but thin clinical proof; Myndlift has both but needs a clinician ($200–500/mo). **[H]**
4. **Five global segments, not one** — optimizers/biohackers, worried-well aging, students, clinical/recovery, longevity enthusiasts — with very different WTP ($5 → $150/mo) and trust needs. **[M]**
5. **Adherence is the make-or-break:** ~**70% abandon within 100 days**; the proven levers are progress you can see, ethical streaks (with forgiveness), implementation intentions, social/coaching, and meaning-tethered design. **[H]**
6. **Reimbursement is a real-but-hard wedge:** Germany's **DiGA** (≈58 apps, insurer-reimbursed, mean €227/90-day course) solved the payer problem Pear Therapeutics died from; FDA De Novo (EndeavorRx) is a credibility asset but commercially brutal (Akili sold for ~$34M). **[H]**
   - *In plain terms:* the strongest revenue model is getting an **insurer or health system — not the user — to pay** for the app as a covered treatment (that's *reimbursement*). Germany's **DiGA** program does exactly this: doctors prescribe approved apps and public insurers pay (~€227 per 90-day course) — solving the problem that **bankrupted Pear Therapeutics** (FDA-cleared but no one would pay). The US **FDA "De Novo"** route (a clearance for a brand-new device type, e.g. **EndeavorRx**, the first prescription video-game for ADHD) gives prestige but **doesn't guarantee payment** — its maker **Akili**, once ~$1B, sold for **~$34M**. *Takeaway:* pursue DiGA/clinical reimbursement as a **credibility play (v2), not near-term revenue.*

## 2. Demand drivers
Aging/dementia fear (55M with dementia today → 139M by 2050; dementia is the most-feared condition over 50; 84% believe they can act but only 13% have asked a doctor — a huge unmet need); productivity/focus culture & AI-era cognitive anxiety; post-pandemic mental-health normalization; the longevity/biohacking movement (Attia, Huberman, Johnson mainstreaming "brain as trainable system"); and a rising **scientific-credibility tailwind** that rewards transparency. **[M/H]**

## 3. Competitive landscape (global teardown) + the white space
*Plain-language version. (Detailed figures and evidence grades are in the Competitor Analysis artefact.) The market sorts into five groups; each owns one slice and leaves the same gap open.*

**Group 1 — Brain-training apps (mental exercises/games).** **BrainHQ/Posit** is the science gold standard (70+ published studies, the large ACTIVE trial, a real attention/acetylcholine mechanism; ~$14/mo) — but it's *only* exercises, with **no body/brain sensors**. **Lumosity** was sanctioned by the FTC and now claims only that you get better *at its own games* (not real-life smarts). **Elevate, Peak, CogniFit, NeuroNation** are polished and engaging but have **thin scientific proof**.

**Group 2 — Neurofeedback / hardware (gadgets that read your brain or body and give live feedback).** **Muse** (a brainwave-reading EEG headband, ~$250–500 + subscription, 100+ studies; the newer Muse S Athena adds blood-flow sensing; maker InteraXon has raised ~$41M) is the leader. **Mendi** (~$300) relies on self-report; **Sens.ai** (~$1,500) is pricey with trials still pending; **Myndlift** is well-evidenced but **needs a clinician** (~$200–500/mo, so not mass-market). Also **Narbis, FocusCalm, Emotiv**.

**Group 3 — Regulated digital therapeutics / DTx (apps approved as actual medical treatments).** **EndeavorRx** (the FDA-authorized ADHD video-game, maker **Akili**) was clinically proven but **flopped commercially** (sold for ~$34M). **Pear** also got FDA clearance yet **went bankrupt in 2023** because insurers wouldn't pay. **Constant Therapy** (stroke/aphasia) has solid trials. **Germany's DiGA** is the bright spot — ~58 apps that insurers actually pay for. *Lesson: medical approval ≠ a viable business.*

**Group 4 — Wellness / "neuroscience-flavored" apps (meditation, calming audio).** **Calm** (~$210–300M revenue — sources conflict; ~$2B valuation; subs declining) and **Headspace** (~$348M ARR) are large but lean on a *neuroscience vibe* rather than actually training the brain. **Brain.fm** (focus audio, a 2025 RCT), **Endel, Apollo Neuro, Sensate, Othership** round out the calming-tech set.

**Group 5 — The frontier (early/experimental).** Psychedelic-therapy platforms (**Mindbloom, Numinus** — here the *drug* does the rewiring), VR rehab, and AI-personalized training. Investors poured **~$4.8B into neurotech in 2025** (a record $976M quarter in Q1 2026), but mostly into brain-computer *implants* — not consumer apps like this.

**THE WHITE SPACE — Limber's opening:** no one offers an **affordable ($15–40/mo), no-doctor-needed, scientifically-honest, personalized** brain practice that does **all three at once**: (a) combines the *full proven recipe* — real training **plus** the healthy-lifestyle levers **plus** sleep/stress — in one place (rivals each do only one piece: train cognition **or** track biometrics **or** meditate); (b) shows progress **honestly, against your own past self** (no hype, no leaderboards); and (c) **optionally** adds a consumer sensor. Each competitor owns one slice — **none owns the honest, all-in-one middle.**

## 5. Users & adherence (global)
**Five segments** (full personas in Stage 7): Optimizers/biohackers (25–45 pros, $30–100/mo, peak performance); Worried-well aging (50–70, $15–40/mo, dementia anxiety, demand clinical credibility); Students/learners (16–30, $5–20/mo, focus/exams, price-sensitive); Clinical/recovery (stroke/ADHD/long-COVID, $50–200/mo, need validation+reimbursement); Longevity enthusiasts (35–65, affluent, $80–150/mo, want integration with their biometric stack).
**Adherence science:** ~70% churn in 100 days; what works — visible measurable progress, ethical streaks **with forgiveness** (streak-freeze; "6 of 7" framing), implementation intentions (3.2× maintenance), binary tracking during formation, opt-in social/cohort, human/AI coaching (top lever for aging/clinical), and **meaning-tethered design** (a stated life-goal referenced every session). **[H]**

## 6. UX/UI standards (global, world-class) — summary
Onboarding = value before signup (a 3-min baseline micro-task → instant personal insight). Daily loop = one priority signal → one adaptive 8–12 min session → progress pulse. **Adaptive difficulty held near ~85% accuracy** (validated challenge zone). Progress shown as **30-day trends vs personal baseline** (never population leaderboards — credibility + anti-anxiety). Calm earth-tone + teal palette (never red for alerts); WCAG 2.2 AA + APCA; dark/focus modes; reduced-motion; dyslexia/ADHD-friendly; full i18n/RTL/offline for global reach (EAA/Section 508/GIGW compliance). Ethical engagement only — no dark patterns, no variable-reward compulsion. *(Full design system in the prototype spec.)*

## 7. Regulation & ethics (global)
**FTC (US):** cognition/disease claims need "competent & reliable scientific evidence" — the Lumosity line. **FDA:** SaMD/De Novo path for therapeutic claims (EndeavorRx) — high bar, reimbursement risk. **EU MDR + DiGA (Germany):** RCT for reimbursement; insurer uptake mandated provisionally (solves Pear's problem). **Privacy:** GDPR (EU), HIPAA (US clinical), plus EAA accessibility. **Design rule:** market wellness/training claims (near-transfer, lifestyle support) freely; pursue a *separate* regulated DTx track only where the evidence and reimbursement justify it. **[H]**
