# Personas & Empathy Maps — Limber (Global)
### Evidence-based archetypes for a neuroplasticity-core product · June 2026
> Built on the *Global Market Dossier* (segment data + adherence science) and the *Neuroplasticity Science Core*. Each persona carries a clear problem & customer definition, cited real-data anchors, a full **empathy map** (Says/Thinks/Does/Feels + Pains/Gains), JTBD, a representative voice (synthesized from documented review/community themes), and design implications. ## Persona 1 — "Optimizer Ryo," 34 · Singapore/SF · BEACHHEAD
**Archetype:** the data-driven peak-performer who treats the brain like a trainable system.
**Problem:** he wants a real cognitive edge but is surrounded by hype — gamified puzzles that don't transfer and gadgets that overclaim. He needs something rigorous and measurable.
**Customer:** senior PM at a global tech firm; already runs Oura + Whoop + a Huberman-informed routine; iOS; high WTP.
**Real-data anchors:** biohackers average **$214/mo** on optimization; **77%** adopt for cognitive enhancement; optimizers skew 25–45, NA-led; cognition is the #1 biohacking use-case. The far-transfer null means generic brain games won't satisfy him.

| EMPATHY MAP — Ryo | |
|---|---|
| **SAYS** | "Show me the data and the study behind it." "I don't want puzzles, I want measurable gains." |
| **THINKS** | *Most of this is snake oil.* *If it's not evidence-based, it's noise.* *My edge has always been how fast I think — what happens to me if that slips?* *Everyone my age is optimizing; standing still is falling behind.* *I'll believe a benefit when I see it in my own data.* |
| **DOES** | Reads studies/Huberman transcripts; stacks wearables; A/B-tests his own routines; churns from hypey apps fast. |
| **FEELS** | Driven and competitive, but quietly anxious that his cognitive edge is finite; skeptical to the point of distrust; time-pressured; energized by rigor, insulted by marketing fluff; reassured only by his own measurable progress. |
| **PAINS** | Overclaiming apps; gamified fluff; siloed tools that don't talk to his biometric stack. |
| **GAINS** | Rigorous, cited training; integration with his wearables; honest progress vs his own baseline; real challenge. |

**JTBD:** *When I want a sustainable cognitive edge, I want a rigorous, measurable daily practice that integrates with my data, so I can improve without falling for hype.*
**Design implications:** evidence-transparency front-and-center; wearable/biofeedback integration; honest baseline analytics; the ~85% challenge zone; no overclaims.
> *Representative voice:* "I'll try anything once — but the second an app promises to 'boost my IQ,' I'm out. Show me the effect size."

## Persona 2 — "Worried-Well Margaret," 62 · London/Melbourne · LARGEST EXPANSION
**Archetype:** the proactive aging adult determined to protect her mind.
**Problem:** her husband is showing early memory issues and she's anxious about her own decline — but she's overwhelmed by pseudoscience and doesn't know what's credible.
**Customer:** recently-retired teacher; moderate tech comfort; wants medical credibility and simplicity, not leaderboards.
**Real-data anchors:** dementia is the **most-feared condition over 50**; **70%** of over-50s worry about it; **84%** believe they can act but only **13%** have asked a doctor — a vast unmet, credibility-hungry need; 55M with dementia today → 139M by 2050.

| EMPATHY MAP — Margaret | |
|---|---|
| **SAYS** | "Is this actually proven, or just another fad?" "I want something a doctor would approve of." |
| **THINKS** | *I don't want to end up like my husband — watching him fade is my deepest fear.* *I can't tell what's real online from what's selling me something.* *Was that lost name normal, or the start of something?* *If I'd acted earlier for him, would it have helped?* *I want to do something, but I don't want to be alarmist.* |
| **DOES** | Reads health news; tries and abandons apps that feel trivial or gamey; asks friends; rarely asks her GP. |
| **FEELS** | Frightened by what she's watching at home; quietly grieving; motivated to act but easily overwhelmed by jargon and hype; reassured by calm credibility; ashamed to admit how much the fear occupies her. |
| **PAINS** | Pseudoscience; competitive scoring that stresses her; complex UIs; fear-based marketing. |
| **GAINS** | Credible, simple daily practice; honest framing of what helps; gentle progress; reassurance, not alarm. |

**JTBD:** *When I fear losing my sharpness as I age, I want a credible, simple daily practice I can trust, so I can act on my brain health without falling for hype.*
**Design implications:** clinical-credibility cues (advisors, citations, "what the science shows"); calm, simple, large-type accessible UI; personal-baseline (never population leaderboards); the lifestyle levers framed as doctor-aligned.
> *Representative voice:* "I don't want to compete with strangers. I want to know I'm doing something that genuinely helps."

## Persona 4 — "Recovery James," 45 · Chicago
**Archetype:** the person rebuilding the mind he used to have.
**Problem:** 18 months of long-COVID brain fog has cost him his professional edge; existing apps feel trivial relative to his severity, and he needs validated, progress-evidenced tools (ideally reimbursed).
**Customer:** former marketing director; high motivation; seeks clinical credibility + insurance.
**Real-data anchors:** ADHD apps $1.9B→6.7B (2033); long-COVID cognitive rehab is an emerging need with **no approved standard**; clinical segment WTP $50–200/mo, with **reimbursement (DiGA/DTx) the unlock**.

| EMPATHY MAP — James | |
|---|---|
| **SAYS** | "I need to get back to who I was." "Is this clinically validated? Will insurance cover it?" |
| **THINKS** | *Will I ever be the person I was before?* *These puzzle apps don't take how serious this is seriously.* *I used to run rooms; now I lose my train of thought mid-sentence.* *Is this permanent, or am I getting better and just can't feel it?* *I need proof, not vibes.* |
| **DOES** | Researches clinical options; tries rehab tools; tracks symptoms; wants provider involvement. |
| **FEELS** | Frustrated and grieving the loss of his sharpness; determined to recover; frightened it's permanent; his professional identity feels threatened; desperate for credible evidence of progress. |
| **PAINS** | Trivial gamey apps; no progress evidence; no clinical credibility; cost without reimbursement. |
| **GAINS** | Validated, graded recovery program; clear progress evidence; clinical language; reimbursement path. |

**JTBD:** *When brain fog has taken my edge, I want a validated program that shows me I'm recovering, so I can rebuild my cognitive capacity and my confidence.*
**Design implications:** a clinical/recovery track with credible language; honest progress evidence; provider integration; the DiGA/clinical reimbursement path; gentle, graded difficulty.
> *Representative voice:* "I don't want a game. I want proof I'm getting better."

## Cross-persona insight
All six want the same thing the category fails to deliver: **a credible, integrated way to actually train the mind** — honestly measured, fitted to them. The far-transfer null and the Lumosity precedent mean the winner is whoever pairs real, specific training (the science core) with radical evidence-honesty. The neuroplasticity lens explains *why* the loop works: attended, adaptive, spaced practice on top of the lifestyle levers.
