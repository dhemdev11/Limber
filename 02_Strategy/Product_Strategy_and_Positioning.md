# Product Strategy & Positioning — Limber
### The science of a more adaptable mind · Global · Neuroplasticity-core · June 2026
> *Limber — adj. able to bend and adapt with ease. A limber mind is a trainable one.* This rewrites the strategy for the global, neuroplasticity-core scope. Every choice is grounded in the *Neuroplasticity Science & Product Core* and the *Global Market Dossier*.

---

## 1. The decision: neuroplasticity as the core, honesty as the moat
**Positioning statement:**
> **For** anyone who wants a sharper, more adaptable mind — from focused professionals to people facing aging — **Limber is** a daily neuroplasticity practice **that** trains your brain the way the science actually says it changes: attended, adaptive, spaced practice plus the lifestyle levers that gate plasticity — measured honestly against your own baseline — **unlike** brain-training apps that overclaim (and get fined) or wellness apps that ride a neuroscience halo they can't back.

**Why neuroplasticity is the core — and why it holds up:** Your brain can change at any age. That isn't a slogan; it's settled science. But here's the part the industry quietly skips: that change is *earned*. It's specific, it takes real effort, and it's slow — there are no shortcuts, only a recipe. And the recipe is well-established: decades of research (captured in **Kleim & Jones' 10 principles** of how the brain rewires) plus the handful of things that genuinely move the needle — **focused practice, exercise, sleep, managing stress, and novelty.** The problem is that no one puts the *whole* recipe in one honest place. Brain-game apps drill puzzles but ignore your body and your sleep. Brainwave headbands give you feedback, but thin proof it lasts. Meditation apps borrow the language of neuroscience without the substance. Biohacking gadgets simply overpromise. Everyone owns one slice — **Limber owns the honest middle: the whole recipe, in one place, with nothing oversold.**

**Why honesty is the moat, not a constraint:** the category's defining event is Lumosity's **$2M FTC fine** for overclaiming, and a 70-scientist consensus calling industry marketing "exaggerated." In a market of inflated claims and a more sophisticated, science-literate consumer (the Huberman/Attia effect), **radical evidence-honesty is a differentiator competitors structurally can't copy without abandoning their claims.** Limber says "improves the skills you train" and "supports brain health," never "raises your IQ" or "rewires your brain in 21 days."

## 2. The core experience: Prime → Train → Consolidate
Limber operationalizes the plasticity recipe into a daily loop:
- **PRIME** — set state & attention (brief regulation + a *personally-meaningful goal tether*; salience is a biological lever, Principle 7).
- **TRAIN** — one **specific**, **adaptive-difficulty** session held near the **~85% accuracy** challenge zone (consistent with desirable-difficulty research), with genuine novelty and real effort (Specificity + Intensity + Attention).
- **CONSOLIDATE** — **spaced** review + **sleep** support + brief reflection (reconsolidation + sleep consolidation).
Around the loop sit the **lifestyle levers** (move, sleep, stress, novelty) as first-class plasticity inputs, **honest 4-week baseline assessments**, **adaptive difficulty**, and **personalization for individual variability** (the BDNF Val66Met Met allele — carried by ~20–50% depending on ancestry — blunts response, so uniform protocols are scientifically wrong).

## 3. Vision, mission, principles
**Vision:** a world where keeping your mind adaptable is as normal — and as evidence-based — as keeping your body fit.
**Mission:** give everyone a daily, science-honest practice that trains the conditions and skills that make the brain adaptable, measured against their own baseline.
**Principles (from Kleim & Jones + the science core):** attention is the gate (no passive consumption); adaptive difficulty is non-negotiable (the ~85% zone); high & spaced repetition; train specific, named circuits (no general-intelligence promises); meaning & salience drive plasticity; model the individual, don't average; lifestyle levers are part of the product, not add-ons; measure behavior, not just self-report; **claim only what the evidence supports.**

## 4. Global segments & beachhead
**Five segments** (full personas in Stage 7): Optimizers/biohackers · Worried-well aging · Students/learners · Clinical/recovery · Longevity enthusiasts.
**Beachhead:** **the "optimizers/longevity" cohort in North America** (25–55, high WTP $30–100+/mo, science-literate, already in the Oura/Whoop/Huberman ecosystem, reachable via creators & longevity communities) — they pay, they evangelize, and they *reward* honesty. **Expansion:** (2) worried-well aging (largest, dementia-anxious, demands clinical credibility — served via the evidence library + a European **DiGA** track); (3) students globally (scale, price-sensitive, freemium); (4) clinical/recovery via partnerships/reimbursement.

## 5. Business model (global, B2B2C-leveraged)
Freemium B2C: **Free** (baseline assessment + limited daily loop), **Plus ~$12.99/mo · $89/yr** (full adaptive training, lifestyle levers, progress analytics), **Pro ~$19.99/mo** (coaching + deeper personalization); **optional hardware/biofeedback attach** (HRV/EEG integrations); **B2B employer** wellbeing/cognitive-fitness ($4–8 PEPM) as the scale engine; a **European DiGA / clinical** track for credibility + reimbursement. (Full TAM/SAM/SOM & BMC in Stage 4.)

## 6. Differentiation & moat
1. **The only integrated, honest plasticity stack** (deliberate-practice training + lifestyle levers + sleep/stress, in one loop).
2. **Evidence-honesty as brand** — personal-baseline progress, a public "what the science does and doesn't show" library, named advisors, no far-transfer claims. A trust moat.
3. **Personalization for variability** — adapts to the individual's response (the science *requires* it; competitors average).
4. **World-class, accessible, global UX** — the ~85% challenge zone, ethical engagement (no dark patterns), WCAG 2.2 AA, full i18n/offline.
5. **Optional regulated/DiGA track** — credibility and a reimbursement path most consumer apps can't access.

## 7. Risks named (not hidden)
| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Far-transfer / overclaiming (FTC) | **Existential** | Claims policy hard-wired; near-transfer + lifestyle framing only; legal review; cite every claim |
| Adherence (~70% churn) | **Existential** | Visible progress, ethical streaks w/ forgiveness, implementation intentions, coaching, meaning-tether; prove in pilot |
| Efficacy proof vs incumbents (BrainHQ) | High | Build the evidence program early; publish; partner academically; honest framing as the wedge |
| Commercial DTx trap (Pear/Akili) | High | Lead consumer/B2B; pursue DiGA only where reimbursement is real |
| Trust in a hype-saturated category | High | Radical transparency is the brand, not a disclaimer |
| Global complexity (i18n, regulation) | Medium | Build i18n/accessibility from day one; phased market rollout |

## 8. North-star & guardrail metrics
**North star:** *Weekly Trained-and-Consolidated Sessions* (sessions completed in the challenge zone **and** consolidated) — captures real plasticity dose, not vanity engagement.
**Guardrails:** Day-30 / Day-90 retention vs the ~70%-churn floor; % sessions held in the ~85% zone; measured baseline improvement on trained domains (near-transfer, honestly reported); lifestyle-lever adherence; NPS/trust; B2B utilization.
