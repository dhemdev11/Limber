# Competitor Analysis — Limber (Global, Neuroplasticity)
### Brain-training, neurofeedback, digital therapeutics & neuro-wellness · June 2026
> Rebuilt for the global, neuroplasticity-core scope. Cited; evidence grades and overclaim risk flagged. Identifies the white space Limber owns.

## 1. The five competitive categories
| Category | Examples | Core value | The gap Limber exploits |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cognitive/brain-training** | BrainHQ, Lumosity, CogniFit, Elevate, Peak, NeuroNation | Adaptive cognitive exercises | Train cognition in isolation; far-transfer debate; **no biofeedback**; no lifestyle levers |
| **Neurofeedback/hardware** | Muse, Mendi, Sens.ai, Myndlift, Narbis, Emotiv | Real-time brain-state feedback | Thin clinical proof (or clinician-gated & costly); narrow (state, not skill) |
| **Regulated DTx** | EndeavorRx/Akili, Pear (defunct), Constant Therapy, DiGA apps | RCT-backed, condition-specific | Narrow indications; reimbursement-dependent; commercially brutal |
| **Neuro-wellness/audio** | Calm, Headspace, Brain.fm, Endel, Apollo, Sensate | Calm/focus, mindfulness | Neuroscience *halo* without the training/plasticity substance |
| **Frontier** | Mindbloom/Numinus, VR neuro-rehab, AI training | Psychedelic integration, immersive | The drug/hardware does the work; no honest, accessible daily practice |

## 2. Hard-numbers profiles (with evidence grade & overclaim risk)
- **BrainHQ / Posit** — *evidence leader* (70 papers in 2025, ACTIVE trial, ACh-upregulation mechanism). $14/mo, $96/yr. **B+; overclaim LOW** but **no biofeedback, no lifestyle stack.**
- **Lumosity** — FTC **$2M** settlement; near-transfer only; ~$12/mo. **D; overclaim HIGH historically.** The category's cautionary tale.
- **CogniFit / Elevate / Peak / NeuroNation** — engagement-led gamified training; sparse independent RCTs. **C/D; overclaim MEDIUM.**
- **Muse (InteraXon)** — consumer EEG; 100+ studies; Muse S Athena adds fNIRS; ~$250–500 + ~$13/mo; total funding ~$41M (last round ~$9.5M Series C, 2022). **B−; overclaim MEDIUM** (biofeedback real; lasting-change claims extrapolated).
- **Cambridge Brain Sciences / Creyos** — scientifically-credentialed cognitive *assessment* platform (Owen lab pedigree); B2B/enterprise & health systems. **B+; overclaim LOW.** The most direct credibility threat to Limber's evidence-honest positioning — counter by pairing assessment with the *training + lifestyle* stack it lacks.
- **Mendi** — fNIRS, $300, self-report only. **C.** **Sens.ai** — $1,500, 5 modalities, trials pending. **C−; overclaim MED-HIGH.** **Myndlift** — clinician-gated, evidence-solid, $200–500/mo (gates mass market). **B−.**
- **EndeavorRx / Akili** — FDA De Novo, RCT-backed (near-transfer on attention test). **A−; overclaim LOW** — but commercially collapsed (acquired 2024 by Virtual Therapeutics via all-cash tender, ~$34M deal value — far below its 2021 SPAC valuation). **Pear** — FDA-cleared, **bankrupt 2023** (reimbursement, not evidence, killed it). **Constant Therapy** — stroke/aphasia, RCTs. **B.**
- **German DiGA** — ~58 insurer-reimbursed apps; mean €227/90-day. **C–B; overclaim LOW** (RCT-gated).
- **Calm / Headspace** — Calm ~$210–300M rev (2025 est.; the ~$596M Latka figure is disputed, subs declining) / Headspace ~$348M ARR; mindfulness *halo*; company-funded RCTs (COI). **C/C+; overclaim MEDIUM.** **Brain.fm** — neural phase-locking, 2025 RCT (n=212). **C+.** **Apollo/Sensate/Endel** — autonomic/audio devices; pilot data; **C/C−; overclaim MED-HIGH.**
- **Platform players — Apple / Samsung / Google** — building cognitive-health & mindfulness features into the OS/wearable layer (the #2 platform-encroachment risk). **Profile: enormous distribution, shallow depth, no honest plasticity stack** — counter with depth, the evidence program, and personalization they won't build.

## 4. Positioning map (two axes)
- **X: Evidence-honest ↔ Halo/overclaim** · **Y: Single-mechanism ↔ Integrated plasticity stack.**
- BrainHQ: honest, single-mechanism (cognition). Muse: mid, single (state). Calm/Headspace: halo, single (mindfulness). EndeavorRx: honest, single (clinical). **Limber: most evidence-honest AND most integrated** — an unoccupied corner.

## 5. The white space Limber owns
A **$15–40/mo, no-clinician, clinically-honest, personalized neuroplasticity practice** that integrates the *full validated stack* (adaptive deliberate practice + the lifestyle levers + sleep/stress), shows progress against a personal baseline, optionally layers biofeedback, and is built to global accessibility standards. BrainHQ has evidence but no feedback or lifestyle stack; Muse has feedback but thin proof; Myndlift has both but needs a clinician. **No incumbent occupies the honest, integrated, accessible middle.**

## 6. Competitive risks & responses (with triggers & concrete actions)
- **BrainHQ adds biofeedback or a lifestyle layer** → *Trigger:* public roadmap/launch. *Action:* accelerate the integrated lifestyle-stack + wearable differentiators and publish a first near-transfer RCT within 18 months; lock the transparency-brand lead.
- **Creyos/Cambridge Brain Sciences moves consumer/D2C** → *Trigger:* consumer SKU. *Action:* pair our assessment with the *training + lifestyle* stack they lack; co-opt their credibility narrative with our published evidence.
- **Platform players (Apple/Samsung/Google) ship cognitive-fitness features** → *Trigger:* OS/wearable feature. *Action:* go deep where they stay shallow (adaptive specificity, personalization, the evidence program); pursue integration *with* their wearables rather than competing on distribution.
- **Regulatory/efficacy scrutiny (FTC)** → *Trigger:* inquiry or category action. *Action:* the claims policy + published evidence are the defense; never overclaim; the honesty posture converts scrutiny into a trust advantage.
