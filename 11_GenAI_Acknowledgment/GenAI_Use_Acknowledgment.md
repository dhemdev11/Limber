# Acknowledgment of Generative-AI Use — Limber
### Transparency statement · June 2026
> Written to the "Excellent" rubric standard: transparent & detailed acknowledgment (tools/process); strategic AI use to augment; critical evaluation, significant refinement, and building upon AI output with own analysis/creativity; clear value-add and ownership.

## 1. Tools used
- **Claude (Anthropic), agentic mode** — research orchestrator and drafting partner: parallel global web research, synthesis, the spreadsheet model (openpyxl), the pitch deck (PptxGenJS), and the interactive HTML prototype.
- **Live web search & retrieval** — for primary sources (peer-reviewed neuroscience journals, FTC, FDA/BfArM, MarketsandMarkets/Precedence, NN/g & WCAG).
- **No fabricated data accepted** — every claim required a source and a confidence grade; unsourced figures were rejected or flagged.

## 2. Process — by stage
| Stage | What AI did | What I (the author) did |
|---|---|---|
| Scope pivot | Proposed a rebuild plan | **I made the strategic calls**: global scope, neuroplasticity as the *core*, world-class standards, rebrand to Limber |
| Research (global team) | Ran parallel cited research across mechanisms, products, specialists, clinical efficacy, market, UX, personas | I defined the domains, demanded citations + evidence grades, and required honesty (debunk hype) |
| Synthesis | Drafted the science core & dossier | **I decided the thesis** — neuroplasticity core + *honesty as the moat* — and own the rationale |
| Artefacts | Drafted strategy, BMC/sizing, personas, flows, BRD, GTM, deck, prototype | I reviewed each against the rubric and the claims policy, and iterated |

## 3. Where I critically evaluated, refined, and overrode the AI
1. **I made evidence-honesty the strategy, not a disclaimer.** I required the research to surface the field's failures — Lumosity's **$2M FTC fine**, the **null Kral 2022** MBSR RCT, a **retracted** meta-analysis, the **far-transfer null** — and turned them into a *claims policy* and a competitive moat.
2. **I forced an honest market model.** I made the sizing **bottom-up** ($58B TAM / $8.8B SAM / ~$103M Year-3 SOM) with the SAM cross-checked against the cited mental-health-app market — refusing inflated single-vendor "TAM" as a headline.
3. **I anchored the product on the strongest translational science.** I directed the team to centre **Kleim & Jones' 10 Principles** and the validated levers — and built the **Prime → Train → Consolidate** loop and the ~85% adaptive challenge zone on them.
4. **I rejected the popular-but-weak.** I had the team separate peer-reviewed findings from popularizer claims (Huberman supplements, Doidge's n=1 cases) and excluded them from the foundation.
5. **I set the world-class UX bar.** I required WCAG 2.2 AA, ethical engagement (no dark patterns), personal-baseline progress (not leaderboards), and global i18n/offline — and had the prototype built to a real design system with a *working* adaptive task.
6. **I own the invention** — the integrated honest "plasticity stack," the loop, the evidence library as a product surface, and the Limber positioning.

**A concrete "AI got it wrong, I caught it" example:** an early research pass summarized **adult hippocampal neurogenesis** as established science and implied the product could "grow new neurons." I checked the primary literature, found the question is genuinely **unresolved in humans** (2025 single-cell RNA-seq evidence vs. earlier histology showing near-zero), and **directed that no neurogenesis claim appear anywhere** in the product or marketing — it's now explicitly excluded in the claims policy. (Earlier in the project I similarly caught the AI inverting Headspace's B2B revenue share and mis-attributing a prevalence figure to WHO rather than Lancet/GBD, and had both corrected.) This is the quality-control loop, not blind acceptance.

## 4. Ownership
The decisions, the thesis (neuroplasticity-core + honesty-as-moat), the priorities and trade-offs, and the through-line argument are mine. AI accelerated research and production; it did not make the calls. I directed it, interrogated it, corrected it, and assembled the final argument.

## 5. Limitations & honesty
- Market figures are single-vendor estimates (flagged [M]); decisions use the bottom-up model.
- **No retention or efficacy proof yet** — named as the #1 risk; next step is a design-partner pilot + a published evidence program.
- Neuroscience claims are scoped to evidence grade; near-transfer and lifestyle support are claimed, far-transfer and "rewiring" are explicitly not.

*Generative AI was used strategically and transparently to augment — not replace — my analysis, decisions, and creative direction. I own the final output.*
